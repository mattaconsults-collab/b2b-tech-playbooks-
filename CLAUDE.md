# Project Purpose

This repository is used to research emerging B2B technologies and create:
1. Credible marketing playbooks
2. Comparison reports
3. Deck outlines
4. LinkedIn content that generates conversations and inbound interest

The primary audience is B2B marketing leaders, founders, demand generation leaders, and creative teams evaluating new technology.

# Author Context

The author is Matt, an Account Executive at Lemonlight, a video production company.
Matt also works with HeroVideo.ai, a software platform related to video creation workflows.

When HeroVideo is included in any analysis, clearly disclose the affiliation.

# Writing Style

Write in a confident, practical, commercially aware voice.
Avoid hype, vague futurism, and generic AI language.
Do not use em dashes.
Be direct, useful, and opinionated without sounding overly promotional.
Prioritize practical takeaways for B2B marketers.

# Research Rules

For every company included in a report:

- Confirm the official company website.
- Explain what the product does in plain English.
- Identify the primary buyer or user.
- Identify the strongest differentiator.
- Identify where the product fits in the marketing or video production workflow.
- Find founded year when publicly verifiable.
- Find employee count only from a credible current source, such as LinkedIn company headcount or a reliable news article.
- Find funding only from credible sources such as official company announcements, Reuters, TechCrunch, Crunchbase, PitchBook, CNBC, The Wall Street Journal, or comparable sources.
- Record the date each source was accessed.
- Never invent a number.
- If information is unavailable, write "Not publicly disclosed" or "Unable to verify from public sources."
- Cite sources for all factual company claims.

# Evaluation Framework

Score or assess each company based on:

1. Primary use case
2. Best-fit customer
3. Video workflow stage served
4. Level of creative control
5. Suitability for B2B marketing
6. Differentiator
7. Potential limitation
8. Funding and company maturity
9. Human production services it may complement or replace

# Workflow

For each project:

Phase 1: Research
- Review the project brief.
- Validate the candidate company list.
- Recommend additions or removals where the tools do not fit the category.
- Produce a sourced comparison matrix.
- Stop and request approval before writing the report.

Phase 2: Narrative Draft
- After approval, create a playbook draft and deck outline.
- Clearly separate facts from analysis and opinion.
- Stop and request approval before creating promotional social content.

Phase 3: Distribution Drafts
- After approval, create LinkedIn post options and a call-to-action.
- Never publish, email, or distribute anything without explicit approval.

# Output Standards

Every finished playbook must include:

- Title
- Executive summary
- Why this category matters now
- Comparison table
- Individual company profiles
- Strategic takeaways for B2B marketers
- Recommended use cases
- Disclosure section
- Sources section
- LinkedIn promotion draft
